THE LIBRARY OF BABEL
U

The methodical task of writing distracts me from the present state of men. The certitude that everything has
been written negates us or turns us into phantoms. I know of districts in which the young men prostrate
themselves before books and kiss their pages in a barbarous manner, but they do not know how to decipher a
single letter. Epidemics, heretical conflicts, peregrinations which inevitably degenerate into banditry, have
decimated the population. I believe I have mentioned suicides, more and more frequent with the years.
Perhaps my old age and fearfulness deceive me, but I suspect that the
